why stephen sanchez

the new sensation

Stephen Christopher Sanchez (born November 3, 2002) is an American singer-songwriter. Based in Nashville, Sanchez released his debut extended play What Was, Not Now in October 2021. On September 1, 2021, Sanchez released the single "Until I Found You", which peaked at No. 23 on the Billboard Hot 100, No. 8 on the Australian ARIA Chart and No. 14 on the UK Singles Chart.

Sanchez's style is very reminiscent of music from the 1950s and 1960s, which is an era of music he has cited heavily influenced him from a young age. His song "Evangeline", released in 2023, contains a sample from Bobby Goldsboro's "Honey", released in 1968.

Hold Her While You Can

Hold Her While You Can is a song by Stephen Sanchez and is the first song from his What Was, Not Now EP.

Release

September 24, 2021

About

The song tells the story of a man who loves his girlfriend and knows that time is precious. He cherishes every moment with her, knowing that one day they will not have the chance to be together. He encourages others to do the same and enjoy the time they spend with their loved ones. Even when he is old and alone, he remembers the love he shared with his partner and holds on to that memory.

Until I Found You

Until I Found You is a song by Stephen Sanchez and is the sixth track on his debut album Angel Face; and the seventh track from his Easy On My Eyes EP.

Release

1 September 2021

About

The song stands out by being a 1950's-inspired love song with old-school charm[1] and bluesy instrumentals creating a swing rhythm. The song went 3x Platinum in the USA (RIAA). Lyrically described as "Sanchez [wearing] his heart on his sleeve", and "a whimsically beautiful love song that causes you to yearn for a time and a love that you never got to experience".

Evangeline

Evangeline is a song by Stephen Sanchez and is the second track on his debut album Angel Face.

Release

25 January 2023

About

"Evangeline" plays a part in the album's larger love story arc, as the album tells the love stories of many different characters between two distinct years. Here, it is about the titular character of Evangeline and The Troubadour Sanchez in 1964. This song is about being completely smitten by someone (Evangeline), but acknowledging that they may not be as eager to fall in love as you are, but you promise them comfort if they fully give you their heart. Overall, it's a heartfelt ode to true love and the lengths that the narrator is willing to go to keep them safe and comfortable.
